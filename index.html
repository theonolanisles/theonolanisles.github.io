Assistive device · Open-source project

Wireless Call Button for Home Care

I designed and built this call button for my father while he was receiving care at home. It provided a simple and accessible way for him to call for assistance when learning new things and communicating became difficult for him.

Add a photograph of the completed call button here.

The problem

A conventional phone or verbal call was not always a practical way to attract a carer’s attention. The device needed to be simple to operate, easy to locate and dependable.

The design

The system uses a large, accessible push button and a separate receiver or alarm unit. The enclosure and controls were selected to make the device straightforward to understand and use.

Design priorities

Simplicity, reliability, accessible operation, readily obtainable parts and straightforward assembly.

Project files

I intend to release the design files so that other carers and families can build or adapt the device for their own circumstances.

Important: This is an independently developed personal project, not a certified medical alarm or emergency-call system. Anyone reproducing it should assess whether it is suitable for their circumstances and should not rely upon it as the sole means of obtaining emergency assistance.
